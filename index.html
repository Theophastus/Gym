Program Structure

Each training day pairs a primary muscle group with a secondary block. The same-day muscle groups rotate completely between the two weekly sessions, so no exercise repeats on the same training type within a week.

Back
2× per week  ·  Mon (deadlift) + Thu (row)
Chest
2× per week  ·  Tue (flat bench) + Fri (incline/OHP)
Shoulders
3× per week  ·  Tue, Wed (dedicated), Fri
Biceps
3× per week  ·  Mon, Thu, Sat — all different pools
Triceps
3× per week  ·  Tue, Fri, Sat — all different pools
Legs
2× per week  ·  Wed (squat) + Sat (RDL)
Abs / Core
6× per week  ·  Every training day

Exercise Library

Every exercise in the program. Setup and execution tailored for a 5'7" frame. Click Watch Tutorial for a YouTube demo or ExRx Guide for the full form breakdown with muscle diagrams.

◆ BACK
Barbell Deadlift
Glutes · Hamstrings · Erectors · Lats

SETUP Bar over mid-foot, 2–3 cm from shins. Grip just outside legs. At 5'7", bar sits at roughly mid-shin at setup.

EXECUTION Drive the floor away, hips and shoulders rise together. Bar stays against the body. Lock out with glutes squeezed.

  • Chest up before you pull — if the upper back rounds at the start you lose the lift
  • Think 'push the floor' not 'pull the bar' — activates the legs correctly
  • Slack out first: tension builds before the bar actually leaves the floor
▶ Watch Tutorial 📖 ExRx Guide
Barbell Bent Over Row
Lats · Mid-Back · Rhomboids · Biceps

SETUP Hip hinge to ~40°. Grip just outside shoulder width, overhand. Back flat throughout.

EXECUTION Pull bar to lower chest, elbows travel back close to the torso. Torso angle stays fixed — no standing up to swing.

  • Lead with elbows, not hands
  • Do not stand out of the hinge during the pull
  • Bar should touch or nearly touch you at the top of every rep
▶ Watch Tutorial 📖 ExRx Guide
Wide-Grip Lat Pulldown
Lats · Teres Major · Biceps

SETUP Set knee pad to hold thighs firmly at 90°. Grip 2× shoulder-width overhand. Lean back 10–15°.

EXECUTION Drive elbows down and back toward hips. Pull bar to upper chest. Slow 3-second return.

  • Initiate with shoulder blades pulling down — not biceps curling
  • Keep chest high and forward the whole set
  • Do not pull behind the neck — stresses the cervical spine
▶ Watch Tutorial 📖 ExRx Guide
Seated Cable Row (Close V-Grip)
Mid-Back · Rhomboids · Biceps

SETUP Low pulley. Feet on plates, knees slightly bent. Torso stays at 90° throughout.

EXECUTION Pull handle to lower chest, elbows back. Full arm extension at the start for lat stretch.

  • Zero torso swing — if you rock, reduce the weight
  • Allow a slight forward shoulder reach at the bottom to stretch the lats
▶ Watch Tutorial 📖 ExRx Guide
Cable Face Pull (Rope)
Rear Delts · External Rotators · Upper Traps

SETUP Pulley at eye level (~63–66" for 5'7"). Rope handle. Stand 2–3 feet back.

EXECUTION Pull rope to face, splitting hands apart. Elbows end at or above shoulder height, beside ears.

  • Elbows must stay higher than wrists throughout
  • Think: try to pull the rope apart as you pull it toward your face
▶ Watch Tutorial 📖 ExRx Guide
Single-Arm Dumbbell Row
Lats · Mid-Back · Biceps

SETUP Non-working hand and knee on flat bench. Working foot flat on floor. Torso parallel to floor.

EXECUTION Pull dumbbell to hip-to-rib level, elbow toward ceiling. Full arm hang at the bottom.

  • Think 'elbow to the ceiling' not 'dumbbell to hip'
  • Do not rotate the torso up during the pull
▶ Watch Tutorial 📖 ExRx Guide
Straight-Arm Cable Pulldown
Lats · Serratus · Teres Major

SETUP High pulley. Stand 3–4 feet from machine, slight forward lean. Arms extended forward.

EXECUTION With arms completely straight, arc the bar down past your thighs. No elbow bend.

  • Arms must stay straight — bending turns it into a pulldown
  • Think of pulling your elbows toward your hips
▶ Watch Tutorial 📖 ExRx Guide
Barbell Shrug
Upper Traps

SETUP Bar at hip height. Shoulder-width overhand or mixed grip. Arms straight throughout.

EXECUTION Elevate shoulders straight up toward ears. No rolling. Hold peak 1–2 sec. Full drop between reps.

  • Straight up, straight down — no rolling the shoulders
  • Full stretch at the bottom every rep
▶ Watch Tutorial 📖 ExRx Guide
◆ CHEST
Barbell Flat Bench Press
Pecs · Anterior Delts · Triceps

SETUP Standard bench, eyes under bar. Grip 1.5–2× shoulder-width. For 5'7": ~70–75 cm between index fingers.

EXECUTION Lower to lower chest, touching lightly. Press in a slight arc toward upper chest. Shoulder blades retracted throughout.

  • Retract shoulder blades before every set
  • Bar touches just below the nipple line for a 5'7" frame
  • Elbows at 60–75° from torso — not flared to 90°
▶ Watch Tutorial 📖 ExRx Guide
Incline Dumbbell Press
Upper Pecs · Anterior Delts · Triceps

SETUP Bench at 30°. 30° is optimal for upper chest without over-recruiting front delt. Kick dumbbells up from knees.

EXECUTION Press up and very slightly inward. Elbows drop below bench plane at the bottom for full upper chest stretch.

  • Above 45° = mostly front delt. Stay at 30°
  • Allow elbows to drop below the bench at the bottom
▶ Watch Tutorial 📖 ExRx Guide
Cable Chest Fly (Mid)
Pectorals · Anterior Delts

SETUP Pulleys at chest height (~48–52" for 5'7" standing). Step forward, slight forward lean.

EXECUTION Arc hands together in front of chest. At peak, actively squeeze by trying to push hands into each other.

  • Think of hugging a large tree — it's an arc, not a row
  • Keep slight elbow bend (20°) throughout — never straight
▶ Watch Tutorial 📖 ExRx Guide
Machine Chest Press
Pecs · Triceps · Anterior Delts

SETUP Seat height: handles at mid-chest (nipple line) when seated. Machine pivot aligns with shoulder joint edge.

EXECUTION Press forward to near-full extension. Return until elbows are slightly behind the chest pad.

  • Seat too low = shoulders too high, front delt takes over
  • Never let the stack touch between reps — keep tension on
▶ Watch Tutorial 📖 ExRx Guide
Smith Machine Incline Press
Upper Pecs · Anterior Delts

SETUP Bench at 30° inside Smith. Position bench so bar travels down to upper chest — not neck or lower chest.

EXECUTION Lower in a straight vertical line (Smith guides the path). Touch upper chest on every rep.

  • Position the bench first, then yourself
  • If bar lands on the neck, move the bench back
▶ Watch Tutorial 📖 ExRx Guide
Flat Dumbbell Press
Pecs · Triceps · Anterior Delts

SETUP Flat bench. Kick dumbbells up from knees. Shoulder blades retracted against pad.

EXECUTION Press up in converging arc. Allow elbows to drop slightly below bench line at the bottom.

  • Slight inward arc at top maximizes pec contraction
  • Shoulder blades must stay pinched together throughout
▶ Watch Tutorial 📖 ExRx Guide
◆ SHOULDERS
Standing Barbell Overhead Press
Anterior Delts · Lateral Delts · Triceps

SETUP Rack hooks at ~54–56" for 5'7". Bar at collarbone. Grip just outside shoulder width.

EXECUTION Press straight up, tilting head back briefly as bar passes the face. At lockout, bar is directly over the ears. Lower to collarbone.

  • Do not press forward. Move your head, not the bar
  • Squeeze glutes and brace core before every rep to prevent lower back arch
  • The bar path should be perfectly vertical
▶ Watch Tutorial 📖 ExRx Guide
Machine Shoulder Press
Anterior Delts · Lateral Delts · Triceps

SETUP Seat height: handles at shoulder level when seated (~53–55" from floor for 5'7" on machine). Pivot aligns with shoulder joint top.

EXECUTION Press straight up to near-full extension. Controlled 2–3 second descent.

  • Lower back stays against the seat
  • Stop just short of full lockout to keep tension on the delts
▶ Watch Tutorial 📖 ExRx Guide
Arnold Press (Seated)
All 3 Deltoid Heads · Rotator Cuff

SETUP Bench at 90°. Dumbbells at shoulder height, palms facing you (like top of a bicep curl).

EXECUTION As you press up, rotate palms outward so they face forward at the top. Reverse on the way down. Rotation is continuous throughout.

  • Rotation must be smooth and continuous — not just at the top
  • Use lighter weight than a standard overhead press due to rotation
▶ Watch Tutorial 📖 ExRx Guide
Dumbbell Lateral Raise (Standing)
Lateral Deltoid

SETUP Stand upright, slight 5–10° forward lean. Dumbbells at sides with slight elbow bend.

EXECUTION Raise arms out to sides until slightly above shoulder level. Pinkies slightly higher than thumbs at the top. 3-second descent.

  • Do not shrug as you raise — traps stay down
  • Pinkies slightly higher: this cue maximizes lateral head activation
  • If you need torso momentum, the weight is too heavy
▶ Watch Tutorial 📖 ExRx Guide
Single-Arm Cable Lateral Raise
Lateral Deltoid (constant tension)

SETUP Low pulley (floor position). Stand sideways to machine, cable crossing in front of you. Working arm away from machine.

EXECUTION Raise arm out and up to shoulder level. The cross-body cable provides tension even at the bottom.

  • Starting with cable crossing the body = tension at the bottom — dumbbells can't do this
  • Keep slight elbow bend throughout

Bent-Over Dumbbell Rear Delt Fly
Rear Deltoid · Rhomboids

SETUP Hinge to ~70–80° (nearly horizontal torso). Arms hanging with slight elbow bend.

EXECUTION Raise both arms out to the sides in a wide arc to shoulder level. Lead with elbows.

  • Torso must be nearly horizontal — upright shifts the load to mid-traps
  • The arc goes out to the sides, not up toward the ceiling

Cable Reverse Fly (High Pulley)
Rear Deltoid · External Rotators

SETUP Both pulleys at high position (70"+ from floor). Cross cables: hold right pulley with left hand, left pulley with right hand.

EXECUTION Pull both handles out and slightly down to sides. Arms stay nearly straight throughout.

  • Arms stay straight — bending elbows turns it into a row
  • Pause at peak and squeeze rear delts
▶ Watch Tutorial 📖 ExRx Guide
Smith Machine Upright Row
Lateral Delts · Upper Traps · Biceps

SETUP Bar set to mid-thigh height (~28–32" from floor). Grip overhand, shoulder-width.

EXECUTION Pull bar straight up along body. Elbows lead and reach shoulder height. Hold 1 sec at top.

  • Lead with elbows, not hands
  • The Smith track prevents the bar drifting forward — use this advantage

◆ BICEPS
Barbell Bicep Curl
Biceps Brachii · Brachialis

SETUP Bar at hip height. Shoulder-width underhand grip. Elbows pinned to sides throughout.

EXECUTION Curl bar up in an arc. Elbows may move slightly forward at peak — not backward. Full arm extension at the bottom every rep.

  • Elbows stay at sides or slightly forward at peak only
  • Do not lean back to swing — slight backward lean at peak is acceptable
  • Full extension at the bottom = full range for maximum growth
▶ Watch Tutorial 📖 ExRx Guide
Incline Dumbbell Curl
Biceps Long Head (peak)

SETUP Bench at 45°. Arms hang fully behind the torso at the start. This pre-stretches the long head (outer bicep).

EXECUTION Curl both dumbbells up, keeping elbows behind the torso throughout. Full hang at the bottom every rep.

  • Keep elbows behind the torso throughout — if they come forward you lose the long head advantage
  • This builds the bicep peak more than any other exercise
  • Fully extend at the bottom — the stretch is the whole point
▶ Watch Tutorial 📖 ExRx Guide
Dumbbell Hammer Curl
Brachialis · Brachioradialis · Biceps

SETUP Standing, neutral grip (palms facing each other). Arms at sides.

EXECUTION Curl dumbbells up while maintaining neutral grip throughout. Do not rotate to supinated at peak.

  • The neutral grip targets the brachialis — the muscle under the bicep that pushes the peak up
  • Can be done alternating or simultaneously
▶ Watch Tutorial 📖 ExRx Guide
Cable Preacher Curl
Biceps Short Head (peak contraction)

SETUP Use the steep end of a bench as a preacher pad, 2–3 feet from low cable pulley. Upper arm on pad.

EXECUTION Curl the handle up fully. Hold 1–2 sec at peak. Lower slowly — do not hyperextend at the bottom.

  • Upper arm on pad eliminates all cheating
  • At 5'7", position bench so pad hits just below the armpit
▶ Watch Tutorial 📖 ExRx Guide
Concentration Curl
Biceps (peak isolation)

SETUP Seated, elbow braced on inner thigh. Dumbbell at full arm extension.

EXECUTION Curl up toward shoulder. At peak, rotate slightly so pinky rises (supination). Lower in 3 seconds.

  • Pure isolation — use this to feel and squeeze the muscle, not for load
  • Rotate the wrist outward at the peak for maximum contraction
▶ Watch Tutorial 📖 ExRx Guide
Cable Curl (Low Pulley)
Biceps (constant tension)

SETUP Low pulley, straight bar or rope. Stand 2–3 feet from machine. Arms extended toward pulley.

EXECUTION Curl up to shoulder height. Lower fully. Cable provides tension at the bottom unlike a barbell.

  • Stand close enough that cable stays taut even at full arm extension
  • Constant tension through the full range — feel every degree of the movement
▶ Watch Tutorial 📖 ExRx Guide
Reverse Barbell / Cable Curl
Brachioradialis · Forearm Extensors

SETUP Overhand (pronated) grip, shoulder-width. Use significantly lighter weight than standard curl.

EXECUTION Curl with palms facing down throughout. The range of motion will be shorter due to mechanical disadvantage.

  • This directly targets the brachioradialis and outer forearm
  • Keep wrists neutral — do not let them bend back under load
▶ Watch Tutorial 📖 ExRx Guide
Zottman Curl
Biceps (up) · Brachioradialis (down)

SETUP Standing, underhand grip, dumbbells at sides.

EXECUTION Curl up in supinated position. At peak, rotate to overhand (pronated). Lower slowly in this pronated position. Rotate back at bottom.

  • This is a two-in-one: bicep on the way up, forearm eccentric on the way down
  • Rotate at the PEAK — not during the movement

◆ TRICEPS
Close-Grip Barbell Bench Press
Triceps (all heads) · Anterior Delts · Inner Chest

SETUP Flat bench. Grip ~16–18" apart (shoulder-width or just inside). Not too narrow — that stresses the wrists.

EXECUTION Lower to lower chest with elbows tucked at ~45° from torso. Press to full lockout, squeezing triceps.

  • Elbows must stay tucked — flaring wide shifts load to the chest
  • Actively lock the elbow at the top to fully contract the tricep
▶ Watch Tutorial 📖 ExRx Guide
Overhead Cable Rope Extension
Triceps Long Head

SETUP High pulley, rope attachment. Face away from machine. Rope over shoulders. Lean slightly forward.

EXECUTION Extend elbows forward and up to full lockout. Return slowly, allowing elbows to bend past 90° for full long head stretch.

  • Best exercise for the long head — the part visible from behind
  • Elbows point forward throughout — if they flare, the load shifts
  • Full stretch at the bottom is critical for long head growth

Straight Bar Cable Pushdown
Triceps Lateral Head · Medial Head

SETUP High pulley, straight bar. Stand facing machine 1–2 feet back. Bar at chest height.

EXECUTION Push bar down until elbows fully extended. Elbows stay pinned to sides throughout. Return to forearms parallel.

  • Elbows do not move — they stay glued to sides
  • Wrists stay neutral — do not let them bend back
  • Squeeze hard at full extension
▶ Watch Tutorial 📖 ExRx Guide
Dumbbell Skull Crusher
Triceps Long Head · Medial Head

SETUP Flat bench. Neutral or slightly pronated grip. Upper arms vertical, pointing at the ceiling.

EXECUTION Bend elbows, lowering dumbbells to sides of the head (temples or slightly behind). Upper arms stay vertical. Extend back to start.

  • Upper arms must stay vertical and still — only the forearm moves
  • Lowering beside the head (not straight down) allows better long head stretch

Dumbbell Overhead Extension (Seated)
Triceps Long Head

SETUP Seat at 90°. Both hands grip one dumbbell under the inner plate. Arms fully extended overhead at start.

EXECUTION Lower dumbbell behind head by bending elbows only. Upper arms stay vertical and close to head. Extend to full lockout.

  • Upper arms stay vertical and close to the head throughout
  • Full lockout at the top is important for complete contraction

Cable Tricep Pushdown (V-Bar / Rope)
Triceps (all heads)

SETUP High pulley. V-bar or rope. Elbows at sides.

EXECUTION Push down to full extension. For rope: split the rope apart at the bottom to maximize lateral head contraction.

  • V-bar: more comfortable wrist position than straight bar
  • Rope: splitting apart at the bottom adds extra contraction

Dumbbell Kickback
Triceps Lateral Head

SETUP Non-working hand on bench. Hinge forward, torso nearly parallel to floor. Upper working arm parallel to floor.

EXECUTION Extend elbow until entire arm is parallel to the floor. Palm faces ceiling at full extension. Upper arm does not move.

  • Upper arm stays parallel to the floor throughout — if it drops, reduce weight
  • At peak: arm fully straight, palm facing ceiling

Reverse-Grip Cable Pushdown
Triceps Medial Head

SETUP High pulley, straight bar. Underhand (supinated) grip, shoulder-width.

EXECUTION Same motion as standard pushdown but with palms facing up.

  • The underhand grip activates the often-neglected medial head
  • Use lighter weight than standard pushdown

◆ LEGS
Barbell Back Squat
Quads · Glutes · Hamstrings · Core

SETUP Rack hooks at ~54–56" for 5'7". Bar on upper traps (high bar). Feet shoulder-hip width, toes 15–30° out.

EXECUTION Break at hips and knees simultaneously. Hit parallel minimum. Drive through floor, maintaining torso angle. Lockout at top.

  • Knees track over toes — caving inward is an injury risk
  • Full brace (Valsalva) before every descent — breathe at the top
  • Bar path should be vertical over mid-foot
▶ Watch Tutorial 📖 ExRx Guide
Romanian Deadlift
Hamstrings · Glutes · Lower Back

SETUP Bar at hip height. Overhand grip, shoulder-width. Feet hip-width. Slight knee bend locked into position.

EXECUTION Hinge at hips only — knees stay in their initial slight bend. Bar travels down the front of the legs. Stop at full hamstring stretch.

  • This is NOT a deadlift. The knees stay stationary throughout — hip hinge only
  • Bar hugs the body the entire way down
  • Stop at maximum hamstring stretch, not when the back gives out
▶ Watch Tutorial 📖 ExRx Guide
Leg Press
Quads (standard) · Glutes and Hams (high foot)

SETUP Standard: feet shoulder-width, center of platform. At 5'7", knees should be at ~90° when sled is at start position. Legs B: feet high and wide for glutes.

EXECUTION Lower to 90° knee bend without lower back peeling from seat. Press to near-full extension, no knee lockout.

  • Never lock out the knees at the top under load
  • Lower back must stay against the pad throughout — if it peels off, you've gone too deep

Hip Thrust Machine
Glutes (primary) · Hamstrings

SETUP Pad just above the hip bone (ASIS). Feet on the platform. Shoulder blades on upper back pad.

EXECUTION Drive hips forward and up by squeezing glutes. Hold peak 1–2 sec — straight line from knees to shoulders.

  • The squeeze at the top is the whole exercise — do not rush through it
  • Chin to chest at the top to avoid neck hyperextension
▶ Watch Tutorial 📖 ExRx Guide
Bulgarian Split Squat
Quads · Glutes · Hip Flexors

SETUP Rear foot on bench (17–18" high, correct for 5'7"). Front foot far enough forward that knee stays over foot at bottom.

Lying / Seated Leg Curl
Hamstrings

SETUP Lying: roller pad just above heel. Knee joint aligned with machine pivot. Seated: same knee alignment — greater stretch at start.

EXECUTION Curl toward glutes (lying) or toward back of thigh (seated). Full extension at the bottom every rep.

  • The seated version provides greater stretch due to hip flexion — superior for hamstring length
  • Lower completely at the bottom to maintain full range

Leg Extension
Quadriceps

SETUP Knee joint aligned with machine pivot. Roller pad just above the ankle. Shin roughly vertical at start.

EXECUTION Extend knee to full lockout. Hold 1 sec squeeze at top. Lower under control.

  • Full lockout at the top maximizes quad contraction
  • Controlled eccentric (3 sec down) increases growth stimulus

Abductor Machine
Glute Medius · TFL

SETUP Back against pad. Pads on outer thighs. Pivot near knee height when seated.

EXECUTION Push legs apart against resistance. Hold peak 1 sec. Return slowly — do not let pads crash back together.

  • Sit upright — leaning forward shifts load from glute medius to TFL
  • Slow controlled return = more time under tension

Cable Crunch (Kneeling)
Rectus Abdominis

SETUP High pulley, rope. Kneel 2–3 feet from machine. Hold rope at sides of head.

EXECUTION Crunch elbows toward knees by rounding the spine forward. The motion is a spinal curl, not a hip hinge.

  • Movement is rounding the upper back — not bending forward at the hip
  • If you feel it in the hip flexors, you are hinging, not crunching

EXECUTION Rotate torso away from the machine with arms extended. Rotation from the waist, not the arms. Pivot on the back foot.

  • Arms stay extended — no elbow bending

Hanging Leg Raise
Lower Abs · Hip Flexors

SETUP Use arm support pads on the pull-up machine for 5'7". This eliminates grip fatigue.

EXECUTION Posterior pelvic tilt first (tuck tailbone under), then raise legs to 90°. Lower with control.

  • Tuck the tailbone before raising legs — this activates abs over hip flexors
  • Do not swing — controlled movement only

Russian Twist
Obliques · Transverse Abdominis

SETUP Seated, torso at ~45°. Hold dumbbell at chest. Feet on floor or elevated.

EXECUTION Rotate torso to one side, dumbbell to beside the hip. Return through center and rotate to other side.

  • Rotation comes from the waist — hips stay relatively still
  • Controlled, deliberate movement — do not throw the weight

Plank
Transverse Abdominis · Core Stability

SETUP Forearms on floor, elbows directly under shoulders. Feet hip-width.

EXECUTION Hold position. Actively 'pull' elbows toward feet without moving — this creates active tension, not passive hanging.

  • Hips must not sag or rise — straight line from heels to shoulders to ears
  • Squeeze glutes as well as abs to maintain alignment

Cable Side Bend
Obliques (lateral)

SETUP Low pulley, single handle. Stand sideways to machine. Working hand holds handle, arm extended at side.

EXECUTION Bend laterally away from machine, contracting far-side obliques. Return under control, allowing a stretch toward machine.

  • Motion is purely lateral — no forward or backward component
  • Keep the opposite hand behind your head or on your hip for balance

Program Notes

How to get the most out of this program.

Progressive Overload

The 5-set heavy blocks at 3–5 reps use ~85–90% of 1RM. Add one rep to your total across 5 sets, or add 2.5–5 kg to the bar. Track your working sets every session.

Rest Periods

  • Main compounds (5-set): 2.5–3 min
  • Secondary compounds: 90 sec
  • Accessories / machines: 60–75 sec
  • Isolation and abs: 45–60 sec

Cardio and Fat Loss

Warm-up cardio prepares the joints. The finisher, done after lifting when muscle glycogen is partially depleted, draws more directly from fat stores. HIIT finishers on Mon and Thu create prolonged caloric afterburn.

Nutrition

Target 2.0–2.4 g protein per kg bodyweight daily. Slight caloric surplus on training days to support muscle gain. Rest day (Sunday) can be at maintenance or slight deficit.

Shoulders — Why 3×

Shoulders respond well to high frequency at moderate volume. Wednesday's dedicated block covers all three heads plus traps. Push days add front and lateral volume. The variation prevents adaptation.

Arms — Why 3×

Biceps and triceps each get a completely different exercise pool on each session. Long head/short head emphasis alternates across the week. Saturday's dedicated arm block adds peak volume when the muscles are fully recovered from Monday/Tuesday.
